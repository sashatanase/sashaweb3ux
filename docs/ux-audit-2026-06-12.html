Executive summary

This is a fundamentally healthy site — and that's not a given. The technical accessibility baseline that most portfolios fail is solidly in place: semantic landmarks, a correct heading hierarchy, language attribute, alt text, visible keyboard-focus styles, an active state in the navigation, and zero text-contrast failures. The writing is clear, confident, and consistent in voice, and the case-study content itself is excellent proof of craft.

The one finding that matters most: on mobile, the navigation vanishes entirely — no hamburger, no menu. Recruiters opening this site from a LinkedIn message on their phone cannot reach Contact or Case Studies without scrolling to the footer, and on inner pages they're stranded. Second priority: the case-studies listing — the page doing the heaviest lifting for hiring — renders its metadata with words broken mid-syllable ('PARTICIP ANTS', 'THRESHOL D') at laptop widths, which reads as a bug on the exact page that should demonstrate polish.

Everything else is small and cheap: one study has two different titles, footer tap targets are slim, and a couple of microcopy details could be tightened. Fixing the top two findings would take this from a good portfolio site to one with no visible flaws.
